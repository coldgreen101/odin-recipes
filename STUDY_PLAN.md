# Odin Recipes -- Foundations Study Plan

## No Spoon-Feeding Contract

By working through this plan, I agree to the following ground rules:

1. **I will struggle first.** Minimum 45 minutes of honest effort before asking for help.
2. **I will read error messages.** Every error is a clue, not a wall.
3. **I will explain my thinking out loud** (or in writing) before receiving hints.
4. **I will not copy-paste solutions.** If I cannot type it from understanding, I do not understand it yet.
5. **I will revisit past exercises** during weekly debug marathons to reinforce what I have learned.

## Socratic Tutoring Protocol

When I get stuck on an exercise, my tutor follows this sequence:

### Pause 1 -- Expectation Check
> "Before I explain, tell me: what were you expecting this code to do?"

### Pause 2 -- Error Investigation
> "What error are you seeing? Walk me through your current logic step by step."

### Pause 3 -- Targeted Experiment
> "Try changing ONE thing based on what you just discovered. What happened?"

### After 2-3 Failed Attempts
Only then does the tutor reveal ONE key insight -- never a full solution.

## Weekly Debug Marathon

Every week, pick 5 completed exercises and try to:

1. **Recreate them from scratch** without looking at your old code.
2. **Intentionally break them** in 3 different ways, then fix each break.
3. **Explain to a rubber duck** (or write a comment) what every line does.

## Practice Challenges (Building Muscle Memory)

After completing the recipes project, try these reinforcement exercises:

1. **From-memory rebuild**: Close all files, open a blank editor, rebuild `index.html` and one recipe page entirely from memory.
2. **New recipe, no reference**: Add a 4th recipe page without looking at the existing ones.
3. **Peer review simulation**: Read your HTML as if reviewing someone else's code. Find 3 things to improve.
4. **Accessibility audit**: Can you navigate your site with only a keyboard? Does it make sense with images disabled?
5. **Validation challenge**: Run all pages through the [W3C Validator](https://validator.w3.org/) and fix every warning.
